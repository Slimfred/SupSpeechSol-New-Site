. . . from my life to yours

Why I chose this profession:

In my early life, my grandmother was impacted by a severe stroke which left her unable to speak. It was so difficult to see her so frustrated, and unable to meet her daily needs or simply say "I love you". This experience early on drove me to pursue a career which would empower individuals and families who who had similar experiences to mine. Later in life my father was diagnosed with Parkinson's Disease. As the disease progressed, his communication skills decreased as well. My own, difficult life experiences have become an inspiration. They are now woven through my professional life and have led me to a profound professional goal: to reduce or eliminate communication frustration for individuals and families, throughout every aspect of their own life.

Why Accent Modification?

Do people have trouble understanding you?
Has your accent kept you from success at work?

There are people who react to accented speakers, particularly in professional situations, in a way that distracts others from the main purpose of their conversation. Although well-meaning when displaying an interest in where an person is from, or expressing a positive opinion about an accent, this may change the direction of a conversation and not allow a professional to reach their intended point.

Do you feel isolated because you have trouble communicating?

Studies have proven that an average person will stop asking for clarification after two attempts resulting in a potentially awkward situation. This often leads an accented speaker to speak less causing feelings of isolation and reduced confidence.

How Accent Modification Can Help:

My method WILL NOT strive to “Americanize” a person or erase the cultural aspects of their life. It is more about learning how we produce speech, and how to implement new habits and behaviors based upon the Standard American Speech Model. It is a myth that you need to be taught and acquire many new sounds. There is a great degree of cross over between American English sounds and sounds which already exist in several other languages. Once the key features of an individual’s language which differs from American English are identified, an individualized program will be designed to focus on the habitual changes that will offer the greatest initial success and long lasting improvement. You will leave my sessions with knowledge and awareness about your speech and communication skills and weekly goals that will bolster your success. With my experience, dedication and passion for my career, coupled with my commitment to supporting you along this path, clear and professional speech is absolutely attainable! This will be life changing and it is exciting for me to provide you with this initial platform to jump from.

Throughout our 12 week program, you will learn and apply the nuances of American English, which will ultimately lead to more effective communication. Because speech varies from speaker to speaker, each client's program will be customized to meet your own personal needs. You will steadily work towards reducing frustrating situations and maximizing your success in your personal and professional communication skills. Sessions will take place one time per week and are one hour in duration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Accent Modification FAQ

What is an accent?

Nonnative English speakers use melody, intonation and stress on syllables in words that differ from American English speakers.  In addition, the movements of the jaw, tongue and lips can vary which affects the pronunciation of consonant and vowels sounds in words.  An accent can affect a person's ability to be understood and stand in the way of success both professionally and personally.

What is a dialect?

Those who speak the same language can have variations in the way they speak, both in sounds and word choice which varies regionally.  Some choose to work on dialect to limit differences in their speech in order to assimilate or for professional purposes.

How can an accent or a dialect difference affect communication?

Some individuals feel challenges and frustration communicating because of their accent.  They may find it difficult to be understood which can lead to decreased social interaction. It may hinder an individual's professional expression, and may lead a listener to focus more on the person's accent than what they are trying to convey. This can negatively impact job performance, educational advancement, self-esteem and everyday life. 

What is involved in accent modification training?

The main goal in accent modification training is to help each client speak more intelligibly and be understood by a larger population.  Elimination of an accent or cultural identity is NOT the goal. A client is actually learning how to "Americanize" their speech patterns. After a specific assessment of an individual’s speech, an individualized program is tailor made.

Voice Therapy FAQ

What is involved in this Voice Therapy program?

The first step is an initial consultation to determine the client’s voice modification goals. Then the program consists of a number of hour long sessions either in person or via Skype. The content of the sessions is determined by the goals set during the initial consultation. The length of each program is individualized and generally ends when the client meets his/her voice modification goals.

What is involved in the sessions?

Sessions are individualized to focus on the client’s goals.

Your session may include:

  • Vocal hygiene training
  • Breathing techniques to support a strong, healthy voice
  • Gender based vocal modulation including
    • Resonance
    • Pitch
    • Volume
    • Intonation
    • Articulation
    • Rate/Volume
  • Gender focused language
    • Semantics
    • Pragmatics (social rules of communication) 

How long is the program?

The length of each program is individualized and generally ends when the client meets all goals set jointly with the clinician such as when the client feels the self-perception of their voice matches that of their identity and personality.
